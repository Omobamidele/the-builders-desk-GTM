# Decision Log: [Short Decision Title]

Software teams have a habit worth stealing: they write down *why* they made a big decision, not just what they decided. Six months later, when someone asks "wait, why do we do it this way?", the answer is one file away instead of living in someone's head (or gone, because that person left the company).

This template does that for GTM tools and workflows. Almost nobody does this for sales and marketing systems yet, which is exactly why it's worth doing.

**Decision ID:** DL-[###]
**Title:** [One line. Name the decision, not the topic. "Moved outbound sequencing from Outreach to Smartlead," not "Sequencing tool."]
**Date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]
**Owner:** [Name. The person accountable for this.]
**Status:** Proposed, then Accepted, then later maybe Deprecated or Replaced.
*(Once something is Accepted, don't go back and edit it. If it changes later, write a new entry that says "replaces DL-###" and link back to the old one. That way the log stays an honest record of what actually happened, not a wiki that quietly rewrites history.)*
